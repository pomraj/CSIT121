Pozole

My Favorite Recipe

Pozole is a soup or stew that is typically made with pork, hominy, and chili peppers. It is a dish that is traditionally eaten in Mexico and has a long history dating back to the Aztec people.

There are many different ways to make pozole, but my favorite recipe is a bit simpler and uses only a few ingredients.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb. of pork shoulder
  • 1 can of hominy
  • 1 small can of green chilies
  • 1 onion
  • 1 clove of garlic
  • 1 tsp. of cumin
  • 1 tsp. of chili powder
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions:

  1. To make the soup, start by browning the pork in a large pot or Dutch oven.
  2. Once the pork is browned, add the hominy, green chilies, onion, garlic, cumin, chili powder, salt and pepper. Add enough water to cover the ingredients.
  3. Bring the soup to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for about 1-2 hours.
  4. Serve the soup with some chopped onion, cilantro, and a squeeze of lime juice. Enjoy!
